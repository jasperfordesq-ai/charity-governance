# Irish Source Log

Date checked: 2026-07-08

Purpose: source inventory for a review-ready Irish charity governance product. This log is not legal advice and must not be used to claim CharityPilot gives legal certainty or replaces solicitor, accountant, safeguarding, employment, data-protection, or governance review.

## Source Check Notes

- Official sources were rechecked by web search/browsing on 2026-07-08.
- Charities Regulator direct automated fetches returned 403 for several pages, but official Charities Regulator URLs and snippets were confirmed through search results for the Governance Code, toolkit/templates, Compliance Record Form, Annual Report guidance, and trustee guidance.
- Irish Statute Book commencement table was updated to 18 June 2026 and still shows mixed commenced and not-yet-commenced Charities (Amendment) Act 2024 sections.
- Law Reform Commission revised Charities Act 2009 page was updated to 22 April 2026 and states that changes known to be in force as of 24 June 2026 are reflected.
- Data Protection Commission, HSA, Tusla, and WRC pages opened directly for conditional GDPR, health and safety, safeguarding, protected-disclosures, employment, and equality source checks.
- Implementation should store source owner, URL, section/page note, last checked date, commencement status, and professional-review flags with any legal/compliance content.
- Conditional duties must be modelled as relevance flags. GDPR, employment, equality, health and safety, safeguarding, and protected-disclosure duties may apply because of a charity's operations, workforce, service users, fundraising, or processing activities; they are not universal charity-law duties for every registered charity.

## Product Modelling Rules

- `in_force`: Current charity-law obligations and official regulator guidance that the product can present as current, source-cited requirements or guidance.
- `guidance`: Official guidance, toolkit, templates, or good-practice material. Use for prompts and evidence suggestions.
- `conditional`: Applies only when facts trigger it, for example staff, public fundraising, children services, data processing, public body status, workplace, or regulated activity.
- `not_commenced`: Enacted but not yet in force. Show as monitoring/future-law context and keep out of current approval blockers unless legal review later directs otherwise.

## Immediate Implementation Implications

- Compliance approval should not allow `APPROVED` if any in-scope `NOT_APPLICABLE` or `EXPLAIN` record lacks a non-empty explanation. Draft editing should remain flexible.
- Annual Report deadlines should remain financial year end + 10 months and should be source-cited.
- Exports should include source date, commencement status, missing evidence, missing explanations, and professional-review flags.
- Onboarding should capture conditional triggers before surfacing GDPR, employment, equality, H&S, safeguarding, and protected-disclosure duties as required tasks.
- Product copy must say "review-ready", "source-cited", or "professional review recommended" and must avoid legal-certainty claims or wording that implies CharityPilot replaces professional advice.
